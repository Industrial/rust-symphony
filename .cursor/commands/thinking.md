# Thinking command: apply the right skill at the right time

Use the skills in `.cursor/skills/thinking/*/SKILL.md` during your workflow. Follow this process and pick the **correct skill(s)** for each phase.

---

## Process (when to do what)

1. **Understand** — Clarify the problem and context.
2. **Classify** — Decide what kind of situation this is (clear vs ambiguous vs complex).
3. **Decompose / reframe** — Break down or reframe the problem.
4. **Decide** — Choose among options or priorities.
5. **Act & adapt** — Execute and adjust using feedback.
6. **Stress‑test** — Check risks and failure modes (especially before “done”).

---

## When → which skill (map)

Use this to choose which thinking skill to apply at each step.

| Phase | Situation or need | Skill to use |
|-------|--------------------|--------------|
| **Understand** | Problem is vague, broad, or ill‑defined | `abstraction-laddering` — move up (why) / down (how) to scope and reframe. |
| **Understand** | Open‑ended or collaborative problem | `productive-thinking-model` — six steps from “what’s going on?” to execution. |
| **Classify** | Ambiguous or large‑scale situation | `cynefin-framework` — classify as Clear / Complicated / Complex / Chaotic and pick strategy. |
| **Decompose** | Complex problem, root cause, or scoping | `issue-trees` — MECE breakdown (why/how trees). |
| **Decompose** | Need to question assumptions | `first-principles` — strip to fundamentals, rebuild from there. |
| **Decompose** | Want multiple angles on the problem | `concept-map`, `connection-circles`, or `zwicky-box` for structure. |
| **Decide** | Choosing among several options | `decision-matrix` — weighted criteria and scores. |
| **Decide** | Prioritizing work or impact | `eisenhower-matrix` or `impact-effort` (or both). |
| **Decide** | Hard trade‑off (e.g. speed vs quality) | `confidence-speed-quality` or `hard-choice`. |
| **Act & adapt** | Fast‑changing or iterative context | `ooda-loop` — Observe, Orient, Decide, Act in tight cycles. |
| **Act & adapt** | Feedback loops in the system | `reinforcing-feedback-loop`, `balancing-feedback-loop`. |
| **Stress‑test** | Risk, failure modes, or blind spots | `inversion` — worst case, failure causes, robust alternatives. |
| **Stress‑test** | Checking your reasoning chain | `ladder-of-inference` — surface assumptions and leaps. |
| **Stress‑test** | Second‑order effects | `second-order-thinking` or `iceberg-model`. |
| **General** | Need multiple perspectives | `six-thinking-hats`. |

---

## How to use this

- At the **start** of a task: use **Understand** and **Classify** (e.g. abstraction-laddering and/or cynefin-framework).
- When **planning or designing**: use **Decompose** (e.g. issue-trees, first-principles).
- When **choosing or prioritizing**: use **Decide** (e.g. decision-matrix, eisenhower-matrix, impact-effort).
- When **iterating or operating**: use **Act & adapt** (e.g. ooda-loop).
- Before **finishing or shipping**: use **Stress‑test** (e.g. inversion, ladder-of-inference).

You don’t need every skill every time. Pick one or two that match the current phase and the situation; combine skills when it helps (e.g. Cynefin then issue-trees for a complex problem).
